This is Mustafa. He(middle), his wife Alaa (not pictured) and their kids have been in boise for 4 months now after living in an egyptian refugee camp for 8 years. They are from Syria and fled from the civil war that began 2011. They love Boise and even the winters. I learned that Boise weather is actually very similar to weather near Damascus, where this family is from. Mustafa’s 9 siblings and mother are still in Syria. Alaa was a gracious host and served us delicious Turkish coffee while we talked. Alaa was a hair stylist in Syria and sold her equipment while fleeing. She would love to get back into doing hair. Click the link in bio to help Alaa restart her business with a pair of hair cutting shears or a hairdressing cape.
This is Kusai, he is 10 years old. He was born the year civil war broke out in Syria, near where his family lived. Kusai has no memory of whereSyria, spending the majority of his life in a refugee camp in Egypt. He enjoys playing soccer and reading. His favorite book is Matilda by Roald Dahl. We’ve added a set of other classic children’s books by the same author to the registry. Click link in bio to buy Kusai a new book! Kusai is a pretty quiet guy but when I asked him what else he might like to have, he described a red drone that could fly.
This is Lamis, she is 11 years old. She was born in Syria but lived in an Egyptian refugee camp for most of her life. With not much to do she took up drawing, she showed us her incredible artworks she had made. She also likes reading. One of her favorite things she’s done since arriving in Boise just four months ago is roller skate at an indoor roller rink. She would love her own pair of roller skates in a women’s size 7.
This is Bernard. He is 12 and in 7th grade. He’s sitting with his little brother and sister. Bernard loves to play keeper in soccer matches with his friends. He dreams of having his own pair of keeper gloves (on the registry!) and his own ball. I also added a goal 🥅 to the registry. Bernard loves school. He especially likes math and English.
The is Juma, he is silly and shy. He likes to play tennis with his brother and his friends. I was thinking he might like spikeball, that’s on the registry as well as marvel superhero shirts that he might also like;)
Sala’s son Mfaume lives with his mom and sister. He is 36 and has yet to find work in Boise. Arriving as the pandemic shut down the community has made locating work next to impossible. With only public transportation and without a computer to search for jobs, this is a challenge for the whole family. Mfaume has a kind smile that makes you happy when you look at him. He didn’t ask me for anything except for a computer or tablet to share with his family. Mfaume was 13 when he fled to Tanzania with his mom and sister. Now, he’s 36 in a new country, in a lockdown, still smiling and welcoming us into his home where he helps care for the kids while his sister works. We put some professional clothes on the registry for Mfaume to hopefully wear to a job interview someday soon.
This is Sala and her granddaughter Sofia. Sala was born in Congo Brazzaville, the small country across the River from Democratic Republic of Congo. She left Congo in the 1990s due to mass sickness and high mortality. Without access to healthcare, vaccines or medicine, she feared for her life. She fled 1300 miles with her son and daughter to Tanzania, where she spent 23 years awaiting resettlement, which came in early 2020. Sala’s family needs a computer to look for work and continue learning English. Our goal is to collect twenty five $10 gift cards in order to buy her one. Find the gift cards in the registry at the link in bio. 💚💛❤️
This is Abdalah, he is 10 years old and is in 3rd grade. When my mom and I asked him what he might like, his eyes got big and he smiled and said only one thing, “books”! I chose some graphic novels for the registry since I figured those would be good for someone who is learning a new language. And since his mom said he also needed more clothes, there are some pants and and shirts for Abdalah too. His mom also asked for a kitchen pot, a tablet or a computer.
This is Mhaju, she is 5 and in pre-school. She has also been in Boise since May 2020. She says she likes reading stories about Jesus as well as playing with dolls. She has a new baby sister, who was nursing during my visit so I didn’t get a picture but she was so tiny and cute. Items in the registry for Mhaju include a baby doll, a book of Bible stories, a stuffed animal and some clothing items.
